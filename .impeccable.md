## Design Context

### Users
School students aged 12–16, primarily on iPads during study breaks. Short sessions, touch-first, competitive mindset. They're between lessons — engaged but not deeply focused. Mobile/tablet is the primary viewport.

### Brand Personality
**3 words**: playful, engaging, rewarding

If BittsQuiz were a physical object, it would be a holographic trading card booster pack — glossy, satisfying to open, with a clear rarity hierarchy that makes earning feel meaningful.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark mode primary (gaming context). The current purple/violet palette is directionally correct but currently executes as generic "AI dark gaming" aesthetic. The opportunity is to push further into the *collectible card game* energy — tactile surfaces, clear rarity hierarchy, a sense that rewards feel earned rather than handed out.

**Anti-pattern to avoid**: The current implementation has several tells — gradient text (`shimmer-text`), purple-to-pink gradients as the primary visual language, glassmorphism blur on modals, and both fonts (Plus Jakarta Sans + Space Grotesk) are overused defaults.

### Design Principles
1. **Rewards feel earned** — visual hierarchy should make legendary/rare items feel special, commons feel common
2. **Touch-first** — 44px+ tap targets, swipe-friendly layouts, iPad-optimized spacing
3. **Playful but not childish** — 12–16 year olds don't want to feel like they're using a kid's app
4. **Speed over decoration** — quiz sessions are short; the UI should get out of the way
5. **Dark mode integrity** — every surface should look intentional in dark, not just "white turned dark"
